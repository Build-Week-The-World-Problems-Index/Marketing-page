World Problems Index

Our Mission:

To investigate world issues, rally behind a solution and use technology to solves problems around the world.

Worldwide

City pollution and smog is a big deal. 10,000,000 tons of smog are released into the atmosphere each day. World Problems Index is proud to have donated thousands of bicycles across the world with our free rental system. Just download our app, go to a designated bicycle location in your city, and ride away. Return the bicycle within 24 hours and the ride is free!

Singapore

World Problems Index has worked with city planners in Singapore to introduce "green" apartments. Experts are designing apartments and high rise buildings with the ability to house nature alongside modern architecture in stunning fashion.

Ohio, USA

When a certain factory was breaking codes and not following laws in Cincinnatti, Ohio, a concerned citizen came to World Problems Index for help. We raised issue with the EPA and recieved enough petition signatures for a full investigation. The EPA found that the factory was in fact in violation of codes, and was forced to reduce their emissions.

Georgia, USA

A concerned citizen brought to our attention that street lights were being turned off at night in Atlanta, Georgia. During that time, accidents during night hours increased 250%. World Problems Index was in contact with the mayor of the city in less than 24 hours and the problem was immediately resolved.

Polar Ice Caps

Climate change is something that is always on our radar. Thanks to donations from members of the Index, we have been able to fund cutting-edge climate research in Antarctica, as well as Northern Canada and Alaska.

Montana, USA

When it was brought to our attention that the wolf population was declining rapidly in Bozeman, Montana, we sent a wildlife investigator to discover the root cause of the issue. For months, we followed the trail until we discovered that a local gun club had been encouraging its members to shoot the wolves, which are protected. We contacted a local wildlife ranger and the gun club was fined and forced to shut down.

Solving World Problems

The World Problems Index is committed to solving world problems. We employ the best investigators, researchers, and planners in the world to not only find the cause of the problem, but to research and implement a solution as well. Our website lets you bring your problem to the world stage. If enough support is garnered for your problem, we will personally take on the fight for your cause.

Connecting Humanity to the World

World Problems Index has opened and closed over 1,000 cases since opening in 1999. We do this by allowing average citizens to connect on our platform, rally behind a common cause, and come together to solve the problem. Of the thousands of problems solved by World Problems Index, each one started with just one person raising a concern.
